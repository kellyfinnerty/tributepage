Her Life

  • Born in Massachusetts in 1866 to Irish immigrants, she lost her mother at a young age and her father subsequently abandoned Anne and her siblings.
  • Anne was visually impaired almost all her life.
  • Anen was the valedictorian of her class at Perkins School for the Blind in Boston.
  • She became Hellen Keller’s teacher at the age of 20.
  • She argued with Keller’s parents over the fact that they once owned slaves.
  • Her ashes were placed in the Washington National Cathedral, a great honor.
